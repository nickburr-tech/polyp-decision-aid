BSG/ACPGBI Limited Life Expectancy Polyp Decision Aid — prototype v0.2.3.2

For clinic discussion: calculates age-adjusted Charlson Comorbidity Index, applies the modelled risk–benefit table, and generates copy/paste wording. Not a substitute for clinical judgement, MDT decision-making or informed consent.

Guidance: BSG/ACPGBI guidance on colorectal polyps in patients with limited life expectancy

Scope and important caveats

  • Use only as a decision aid for colorectal polyps in patients where limited life expectancy is relevant to management.
  • Designed primarily for endoscopically visualised/detected polyps. If a lesion has only been diagnosed radiologically, such as on CTC, use extra caution because endoscopic features suspicious for cancer and technical resectability have not been directly assessed.
  • Do not use for established colorectal cancer, hereditary polyposis/genetic CRC syndromes, inflammatory bowel disease, or transient illness/transient frailty.
  • Do not use as a substitute for assessment of symptoms, endoscopic features suspicious for cancer, SMSA/technical complexity, frailty/resilience, antithrombotic indication, patient preference or MDT judgement.
  • Multiple polyps are not explicitly modelled. Using the largest polyp may be a starting point only; benefit may be additive whereas procedural risk is not necessarily additive in the same way.

Cut-and-paste extractor

Experimental convenience feature: this keyword scan may miss diagnoses or tick incorrect boxes because it cannot reliably interpret context, negation, family history, cancer type, severity or resolved disease. It deliberately avoids broad cancer terms such as “carcinoma” because these can incorrectly capture BCC/SCC skin lesions. Always review the Charlson checklist before calculating.

Model assumptions: Estimated cancer and adverse event rates are derived from the BSG/ACPGBI guidance model and are intended to inform discussion rather than predict individual patient outcomes. The guidance table applies to age-adjusted CCI 3, 4, 5, 6 and ≥7. For CCI below 3, this prototype flags that the patient is outside the modelled table and should not generate a RAG recommendation. The estimated 10-year survival percentage from generic CCI calculators is intentionally not shown because it is not required for applying this guidance.

Model table built into this prototype

RAG classification is generated from the guidance definition: green if cancer risk clearly exceeds SAE risk, red if SAE risk clearly exceeds cancer risk, amber if within roughly 25% / numerically comparable. The underlying estimates are modelled and assumption-dependent.

About: Prototype clinical decision aid developed from the BSG/ACPGBI guidance on management of colorectal polyps in patients with limited life expectancy. Outputs are based on published modelling assumptions and should be interpreted alongside patient factors, frailty, comorbidity, polyp characteristics and patient preferences.

Disclaimer: This tool supports but does not replace clinical judgement, MDT discussion or informed consent. No patient information is transmitted, stored or retained by this static browser-based tool.
